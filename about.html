The Story

How is the microclimate affected by our every-day lives?

How about the opposite? When we walk in a crowded area of a city, does it actually affect our health? To what degree do our walking activities and going out to a crowded place affect the air quality of the area? Air pollution in a city is expected, and the traffic impact on the air quality is nevertheless known. For cities to become greener, they are looking into ways to develop their infrastructure, and one of those cities is Melbourne.

However, the degree to which the air quality is affected by merely the crowdiness of a place is not exactly known. Furthermore, studying the air quality in specific locations around the city would give better insights into how the infrastructure of a specific place or street could be developed. Having the data mentioned above, this is exactly what we are trying to figure out: to which extent is the air quality influenced by pedestrians and other microclimate factors, in specific areas of Melbourne?
